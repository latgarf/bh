How Our Risk Management Products Work


First, you should determine what direction of the BTC/USD exchange rate movement would be detrimental to you.
For example, if you hold bitcoins as investmets, then bitcoin depreciation would erode your value, hence you would want to protect yourself against a decrease in BTC/USD rate. On the other hand, if you have borrowed some bitcoins, then an increase in the bitcoin price would make the dollar value of your debt bigger. In this case, you would want to protect yourself from BTC/USD rate increases.

Then, decide how many bitcoins you want to protect.

Our contracts are initiated when you press the "Submit Order" button on the homepage.

You decide the duration of your desired contract, and then specify the expiry date and time.
Please note that expiry time should be specified in the Coordinated Universal Time (UTC) time zone.
Please check now how many hours is the time difference between UTC and your local time zone.

Please have your bitcoin wallet ready to receive payouts and to make insurance fee payments.


The Order Life Cycle

  1. Specify insurance parameters

    On the homepage, in the "Contract Parameters" form, specify your chosen order size, expiry and
    order type (whether to protect from increase or decrease in BTC/USD rate).
    Notice that as you change the parameters, the quoted insurance fee will change in response.

    Also, in this form's respective field, please paste your bitcoin address where you want to receive your payouts after your order expires.

  2. Press the "Submit Order" button.

    After you press the "Submit Order" button, you should see a confirmation that "Your order was received".
    It will also tell you the bitcoin address where to send your insurance fee payment.
    If you don't see such confirmation (yellow section with red text), then please check your browser - JavaScript must be enabled.

  3. Make your fee payment within 5 minutes after pressing the "Submit Order" button.

    Your order is not opened until your fee payment is received.
    If no payment is received within 5 minutes after you pressed the "Submit Order" button, the order is cancelled).

    If you pay a different amount than the quote, your order will still be opened, but the order size will be adjusted proportionally. For example, if the fee quoted to you for insuring 1 bitcoin was 0.02, but you have paid only 0.01,
    then a contract to insure only 0.5 bitcoins will be opened.

  4. Receive a payout when your order expires
    A payout that is due to you, will be sent to your bitcoin address within a minute after your order expires.

Example

Bob has saved 2 bitcoins planning to buy a $1000 bike Saturday afternoon on 23th August 2014.
The day before, he got concerned that if the bitcoin price drops overnight significantly, he would not have enough money to make the purchase.

In order to avoid the possible shortage of cash, he decided to insure his 2 bitcoins against the possible drop in the BTC/USD rate at 12noon on Friday, when the BTC/USD rate was $523.10. To open an insurance contract, he first specified on our homepage, the type of his desired order -"Protect from: DECREASE in bitcoin value vs USD".

Then, he needed to specify his contract's expiry time. Bob lives in New York. He checked that his local time was four hours behind UTC.
He wanted to receive his insurance payout at 4pm New York time on Saturday, 24th August 2014, that is 2014-08-24 16:00 EDT. Therefore, he specified his desired expiry time as 2014-08-24 20:00 UTC. The amount to insure is 2 bitoins. Then Bob copied his bitcoin address from his wallet and pasted into the form, which now looked like this:



Then Bob pressed the "Submit Order" button.
The bitcoin address for his fee payment was displayed as a result.
Bob made his fee payment of 0.038 bitcoins ($19.34) to that address.

When Bob's order expired at 4pm on Saturday, he received a payout of 0.129 bitcoins ($63.40) in his wallet. The bitcoin price at expiry was $491.40. The payout was determined as (523.10 - 491.40) * 2 bitcoins. Bob was now able to convert his 2.129 bitcoins into $1046.20, exactly the value of his 2.000 bitcoins at insurance contract initiation.
If he had not entered into this insurance contract, the value of his 2 bitcoins at 4pm on Saturday would be $982.80, and he would not have enough money to buy the $1000 bike.
